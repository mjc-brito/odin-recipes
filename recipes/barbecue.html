Barbecue

Grill, smoke and savor the flavor - barbecue, the ultimate outdoor cooking experience

Barbecue, often shortened to BBQ, is a method of cooking meat, fish, or vegetables over an open flame or hot coals. This cooking technique originated in the Caribbean and has since spread throughout the world, with variations in style and flavor depending on the region. Barbecue is often associated with outdoor social gatherings, such as backyard parties and picnics, and is a popular cuisine for casual dining. The cooking process typically involves slow-cooking the meat or vegetables over low heat, which results in tender and flavorful dishes. Barbecue is often accompanied by a variety of sauces and sides, such as coleslaw, baked beans, and cornbread.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds of beef sirloin or ribeye, sliced into 1/2-inch thick pieces
  • Coarse sea salt

Steps

  1. Preheat the grill to high heat;
  2. Season the beef slices generously with coarse sea salt on both sides;
  3. Place the beef slices on the grill and cook for 3-4 minutes per side, or until desired doneness;
  4. Remove the beef slices from the grill and let them rest for a few minutes before serving.

In traditional Brazilian churrasco, the beef is typically cooked over charcoal or wood fire, which gives it a unique smoky flavor. However, you can still achieve a delicious result on a gas grill or indoors on a grill pan.

Enjoy your simple and tasty Brazilian barbecue! You can serve it with sides like rice, beans, french fries and salad.
